## Porque compliance é Tier 5

Em 2024-2025, regulação de AI era opcional. Em 2026:

- **EU AI Act** entra em vigor parcialmente (Feb 2025), totalmente (Aug 2026).
- **NIST AI RMF** virou de facto requirement em US federal.
- **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** = first AI Management System standard, sendo adotada por enterprise.
- **LGPD/GDPR** estendido para AI use cases (decisões automatizadas, direitos).
- Setoriais: HIPAA (US), FedRAMP, PCI-DSS — todos adicionando AI guidance.

Sem compliance:
- Multas (GDPR pode chegar 4% receita global).
- Bloqueio de mercado (EU).
- Reputational damage.
- Lawsuits.

AI Engineer Sênior precisa **saber e implementar** compliance pra apps que vai construir.

## EU AI Act — overview

Aprovado 2024, entra em vigor escalonado:
- **Feb 2025**: prohibited practices banidas.
- **Aug 2025**: General-purpose AI rules (transparency).
- **Aug 2026**: high-risk requirements.
- **Aug 2027**: full enforcement incluindo legacy.

### Risk tiers

| Tier | Examples | Requirements |
|------|----------|--------------|
| **Unacceptable** | Social scoring, real-time biometric ID in public, manipulative AI | BANNED |
| **High-risk** | Hiring, credit scoring, education grading, healthcare, infraestrutura crítica, law enforcement, judicial | Conformity assessment, registration, technical docs |
| **Limited risk** | Chatbots, deepfakes, generative AI content | Transparency obligations (disclose AI) |
| **Minimal risk** | Spam filters, AI in games, search ranking | Voluntary codes |

### High-risk requirements

Se seu app é high-risk:

1. **Risk management system** — identificar, document, mitigate riscos.
2. **Data governance** — quality, representativeness of training data.
3. **Technical documentation** — model card, dataset card.
4. **Logging** — automatic event logs.
5. **Transparency** — user informed AI is in use, capabilities/limitations.
6. **Human oversight** — humans can intervene/override.
7. **Accuracy & robustness** — minimum performance thresholds.
8. **Cybersecurity** — protections vs unauthorized use.
9. **Conformity assessment** — pré-deploy validation.
10. **Registration** — EU database of high-risk AI.

### General-Purpose AI (GPAI)

Specific rules para foundation models (Claude, GPT-4, etc.):
- Technical documentation pra developers downstream.
- Comply com EU copyright.
- Detailed summary of training data.
- Plus, "systemic risk" models (large compute): additional obligations (model evaluations, incident reporting).

You probably consumes GPAI (use Anthropic/OpenAI). Provider responsável pelo GPAI obligations. Você responsible pelo your app's use.

### What you must do (typical app)

If your app uses LLM:

**Always**:
- Disclose AI use ("you're chatting with AI assistant").
- Disclose deepfake-style content ("this image was generated by AI").
- Comply with copyright (don't train on copyrighted without license — usually provider's problem).

**If your app falls in high-risk category**:
- Conformity assessment (CE marking-like).
- Register in EU database.
- Full technical docs.
- Human oversight built-in.
- Monitoring + incident reporting.

**Determining if you're high-risk**:
- Healthcare? Yes if affects diagnosis/treatment.
- Hiring/HR? Yes if screens candidates.
- Education? Yes if grades or evaluates.
- Credit? Yes if affects access to credit.
- Public services? Often.
- Internal productivity tool? Usually no.

Document risk assessment upfront.

## NIST AI RMF (US)

Voluntary framework but de facto for US federal + cresceu in enterprise.

### Core functions

**GOVERN**: AI governance policies, accountability, culture.
**MAP**: Context, risks, stakeholders.
**MEASURE**: Metrics, evals, monitoring.
**MANAGE**: Risk mitigation actions, response, recovery.

### Implementation

1. **Govern**:
   - AI committee (cross-functional).
   - Policy: what AI you can build, can't.
   - Code of conduct (data use, ethics).

2. **Map**:
   - Use case → risk level.
   - Stakeholders (users, affected non-users, regulators).
   - Trade-offs (transparency vs performance).

3. **Measure**:
   - Bias evals (demographic disparity).
   - Robustness evals (adversarial).
   - Latency / cost / accuracy.

4. **Manage**:
   - Mitigation when risks identified.
   - Incident response plan.
   - Periodic review (annual or after major change).

NIST publishes profiles per sector (healthcare, financial).

## ISO/IEC 42001:2023

First international standard for AI Management Systems. Similar a ISO 27001 (infosec) mas pra AI.

### Domains

- AI policy.
- Risk management.
- Resource management (data, compute, talent).
- AI lifecycle (design → deploy → monitor → retire).
- Documentation.
- Continuous improvement.

### Certification

Third-party auditor certifies your org meets ISO 42001. Like getting ISO 27001 cert. Useful for:
- Vendor procurement (large enterprise demands).
- Marketing differentiation.
- Internal discipline.

Certification: 6-18 months, $20-100k depending on size.

## LGPD/GDPR for AI

Estende princípios para AI:

### Lawful basis

```
Para processar dados pessoais em AI, you need:
- Consent (preferred for AI)
- Contract (necessary to fulfill)
- Legal obligation
- Vital interest
- Public task
- Legitimate interest (with balancing test)
```

For most apps: consent. Clear opt-in, ability to revoke.

### Right to explanation

User has right to know:
- That AI is being used.
- General logic.
- Significance + consequences.

For automated decisions (loan denial, hiring rejection), full explanation + human review available.

```typescript
// Right to explanation UX
<AIDecisionExplanation>
  <Title>How this decision was made</Title>

  <Actions>
    <Button>Request human review</Button>
    <Button>Provide additional information</Button>
  </Actions>
</AIDecisionExplanation>
```

### Right to deletion

User can request deletion of their data — including:
- Conversation history.
- Embeddings derived (RAG).
- Logs (sometimes; legal retention may override).
- Fine-tuned models trained on their data (hard, often impossible).

Implementation:
```python
async def delete_user_data(user_id: str):
    # 1. Conversations
    await db.delete_conversations(user_id)
    
    # 2. RAG embeddings
    await vector_db.delete(user_id=user_id)
    
    # 3. Logs (anonymize, keep aggregated)
    await logs.anonymize(user_id)
    
    # 4. Model cache (if user-specific cached prompts)
    await cache.purge(user_id)
    
    # 5. Notify user
    await email.send(user.email, "Your data has been deleted.")
```

### Data residency

EU data must stay EU. Implications:
- Anthropic API: regions available (us-east, eu-west). Choose EU.
- OpenAI: ZeroDataRetention + EU region available em Azure OpenAI.
- Self-host em EU regions.

Document residency in privacy policy.

### Subprocessors

Anthropic, OpenAI são subprocessors. Update sua privacy policy:
- "We use [vendors] to process your data."
- Link to vendor DPAs.

## Industry-specific

### HIPAA (US healthcare)

PHI (Protected Health Information):
- BAA required com provider (Anthropic Bedrock, Azure OpenAI BAA available).
- Encryption at rest + transit.
- Access logs.
- Patient consent (specific consent for AI use).
- Minimum necessary principle (don't send more PHI than needed).

Penalties: up to $1.9M/violation/year.

### Financial services

- Algorithmic bias regs (lending: ECOA, FCRA).
- Model risk management (SR 11-7 — Fed).
- Adverse action notifications.
- Model documentation (assumptions, performance).

### Legal services

- Confidentiality (attorney-client privilege).
- Unauthorized practice of law (AI can't replace lawyer).
- ABA Model Rule 1.1 (technology competence).

### Education

- FERPA (US student records).
- COPPA (children under 13).
- State laws on AI in education growing.

### Recruitment / HR

- EEOC guidance (bias in hiring AI).
- NYC AI Bias Audit Law (2023+).
- Illinois AI Video Interview Act.
- Disclosure requirements em hiring.

## Compliance workflow

For new AI feature:

1. **Risk assessment** (5 mins):
   - Affects users' rights/access? → likely high-risk.
   - Processes PII? → privacy concerns.
   - Public-facing? → transparency obligations.
   - Decisions with impact? → explanation requirements.

2. **Document** (1-2 hours):
   - Model card.
   - Intended use.
   - Limitations.
   - Monitoring plan.

3. **Implement safeguards** (varies):
   - User disclosure.
   - Audit logs.
   - Right to deletion mechanism.
   - Right to explanation UI (if applicable).
   - Bias eval.

4. **Approve**:
   - AI committee review (if your org has).
   - Legal review (high-risk).
   - Privacy review.

5. **Deploy**:
   - Monitor metrics.
   - Quarterly compliance review.

## Audit trail

Compliance frequentemente exige audit trail:

```python
# Log every AI decision affecting user
async def log_ai_decision(
    user_id: str,
    decision_type: str,    # "loan_eligibility", "content_moderation", etc.
    input_data: dict,
    model_output: dict,
    final_decision: str,   # "approved", "denied", "escalated"
    confidence: float,
    explanation: str,
):
    await audit_db.insert({
        "id": uuid4(),
        "timestamp": datetime.utcnow(),
        "user_id": user_id,
        "decision_type": decision_type,
        "input_hash": hashlib.sha256(json.dumps(input_data).encode()).hexdigest(),
        "model": "claude-sonnet-4-6",
        "model_version": "2026-01",
        "output_summary": truncate(str(model_output), 500),
        "final_decision": final_decision,
        "confidence": confidence,
        "explanation": explanation,
        "human_reviewed": False,  # update if human intervenes
    })
```

Retention: minimum 7 years for financial, 5+ for healthcare, varies LGPD.

## Bias testing

Required by EU AI Act for high-risk + good practice always:

```python
# Test response disparity across demographics
async def bias_audit():
    test_cases = generate_demographic_variants()
    
    results = {}
    for case in test_cases:
        response = await ai_decide(case)
        results[case.demographic] = response
    
    # Compare outcomes
    rates = {d: positive_rate(r) for d, r in results.items()}
    max_disparity = max(rates.values()) - min(rates.values())
    
    if max_disparity > 0.05:  # 5% threshold
        alert("bias_detected", disparity=max_disparity, rates=rates)
```

Use libraries: **Fairlearn** (Microsoft), **AI Fairness 360** (IBM).

## Vendor evaluation

When choosing AI provider/vendor:

- Compliance certifications (SOC2, ISO 27001, HIPAA BAA available).
- Data residency options.
- Data retention policy (Anthropic: 30 days default, opt-out).
- Training data opt-out (Anthropic: not used for training in API tier).
- Audit log access.
- DPA (Data Processing Addendum) — sign with subprocessors.

Anthropic, OpenAI, Microsoft Azure OpenAI, AWS Bedrock — all have these.
